# WhatsApp Onboarding Messages

Two parts. Part A is the intro you send with the PDF attached. Part B is six short follow-up messages — for if she prefers reading on WhatsApp, or if you're easing her in over a few days.

WhatsApp uses `*asterisks*` for **bold**. Each Part B chunk is sized to copy-paste cleanly into one message.

---

## Part A — Intro message (send with the PDF)

```
Hey [name],

Welcome to StitchPad — really glad to have you on the team.

I've put together a short onboarding doc (PDF attached) so you can get up to speed at your own pace. It covers what we're building, who it's for, what you'll be doing, and how to test the app.

The 60-second version while the PDF downloads:

— We're building a phone app for Nigerian fashion designers, to help them stop losing customer measurements
— You're our first PM. Your headline first project is finding 5–10 real tailors for us to test with
— Everything else (Notion, Figma, app install) is in the doc

Read at your pace, jot questions as you go, and send them through any time.

— Daniel
```

---

## Part B — 6 numbered chunks (optional follow-ups)

Send these one at a time, in order. She can read them on the bus or at home — no PDF needed.

### Chunk 1/6 — What StitchPad is

```
*1/6 — What StitchPad is*

A phone app for Nigerian fashion designers, to never lose a customer's measurements again.

When we surveyed Nigerian tailors:
— 85% have lost customer records before
— 92% still use paper notebooks
— 96% said an app would be "extremely" or "very" useful
— 81% said they'd pay for it

Losing a notebook costs them re-measurements, missed orders, sometimes the whole customer. We're the thing that quietly makes that fear go away.
```

### Chunk 2/6 — Our user

```
*2/6 — Our user*

Picture one specific person:

A solo fashion designer in Abuja or Lagos. Runs the whole shop alone. Works on her phone (62% iPhone, 31% Android). Identifies hard with her brand name — "House of Ayo", "Stitched by Tomi". Gets paid in deposits and chases the balance later. Not a tech person.

The test we apply to every decision: would she understand this in 3 seconds, while standing in a busy market? If no, it's not done.
```

### Chunk 3/6 — What you'll be doing

```
*3/6 — What you'll be doing*

Your headline first project: *find us users to test with.* We don't have a tester pool yet. Recruit 5–10 Nigerian tailors via Instagram, market visits, friends-of-friends, fashion school networks.

Ongoing:
— Be the user's voice — if a screen confuses you, say so
— Review every flow before we ship (copy, buttons, errors)
— Keep the Notion backlog tidy
— Test every release on your phone first
— Watch competitors and bring back what works
```

### Chunk 4/6 — Tools

```
*4/6 — Tools you'll live in*

— *Notion* — your home base. PRD, backlog, design system, survey data, weekly log.
— *Figma* — designs and the component library. You don't need to design — just see what's coming.
— *WhatsApp* — daily chat with me, plus the tester group you'll help set up.
— *The app* — installed on your phone (next message).

I'll add you to Notion + Figma on day one.
```

### Chunk 5/6 — How to test

```
*5/6 — How to test the app*

*Android:* I'll send you an .apk over WhatsApp. Tap → allow "install unknown apps" once → done.

*iPhone:* TestFlight isn't set up yet. For now, bring your phone over and I'll install via cable (good for about 7 days).

The "golden path" you walk every new build: sign up → set up workshop → add a customer → add measurement → create order → mark Ready → mark Delivered.

Anything broken or confusing = bug. Send a screen recording.
```

### Chunk 6/6 — First week + culture

```
*6/6 — Your first week*

— Read the full doc end to end
— Get on Notion + Figma
— Install Android APK + run the golden path
— Read the PRD + survey data in Notion
— Try 3 competitor apps, write a 1-pager
— Draft a plan: how do you find 5 tailors to test with?
— 30-min weekly sync with me

A few rules we live by: warm beats clever, obvious beats smart, ship small beats ship perfect. Ask twice if unsure — it's never annoying.

Welcome aboard.
```
