Customer-cap-reached bottom sheet — 5 variants

Replaces the dismissible "You've reached your customer limit. Upgrade to add more." snackbar with a proper modal bottom sheet. All five use the locked Adire Atelier palette (indigo primary, copper accent, cream-on-dark) and the same primary action (Upgrade to Pro); they differ in how much they explain and how prominent the "swap a customer" alternative is. Phone mockups are iPhone 17 Pro proportions, dark mode.

My pick: Variant D — it synthesizes C's tier comparison + B's mono stat into a single compact card, with the count visible and the swap action correctly demoted. Variant E is the runner-up if you want the Pro benefits list to do more of the selling. A/B/C remain for context.

Variant E — Editorial + benefits + price Chosen

Serif headline reframes as opportunity ("Ready to grow…"). Pro benefits as a three-row checklist inside the elevated card, price shown in Fraunces serif as a premium signal. Star-icon Upgrade button + a text-link "swap a customer" to stay on Free as the secondary path.

How to choose:

  • Pick A if you want the same sheet to also work for the welcome-window-ending warning later — the icon hero scales to that use case.
  • Pick B if you trust that testers already understand the cap and you want the lightest possible interruption. Smallest sheet of the five.
  • Pick C if conversion to Pro is the goal — most prominent price surface, swap demoted to a link, but no current-count visibility.
  • Pick D (my recommendation) — the count `15/15` lives in the head row, Free/Pro shown as a clean two-row list (not a centered bento), price visible as a pill on the Pro row, single primary CTA, swap as a soft text link. Most compact of the "explanatory" variants.
  • Pick E if you want Pro to sell itself — three-row benefits checklist inside an elevated card, serif price as a premium signal. Tallest sheet; works best when the user has rarely or never seen the value-prop yet.

Once you pick a direction, implementation is ~80 lines of Compose using the existing ModalBottomSheet pattern (mirrors SwapSheet.kt). Replaces the current ShowSnackbar(UiText.StringResourceText(customer_cap_reached)) event in CustomerFormViewModel with a ShowCapReachedSheet event.
