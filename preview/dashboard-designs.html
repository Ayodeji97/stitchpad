Six dashboards + a Reports screen

The shipped V1 is an operational stack. This page compares it against five alternatives — operator, metric-grid, timeline, hero-action, photo-first, and a finance-hybrid (V6) that keeps the operational stack but adds an ambient revenue line. Below the six phones is a separate mockup of a proper Reports tab — the place where weekly/monthly sales metrics actually belong. Same sample data across every phone. Toggle light/dark on any phone with the button below its caption.

auto_awesomeMy recommendation

Ship V6 (Finance-hybrid) once Reports exists. Until then, keep V1.

V1 and V6 answer the same question — "What needs me first?" — V6 just adds a small ambient revenue line at the bottom. That line only earns its place if tapping it goes somewhere real, which means the dashboard and the Reports screen ship together. Until Reports exists, V6 is a dead link; V1 is honest.

  • V1 (Operational Stack, shipped) — the right baseline. Honest, cheapest to maintain, solves the urgent problem.
  • V2 (Metric Grid) — downgrade for a solo tailor. Counts without names/garments means tapping a tile before you know what's in it. Better for multi-staff shops where numbers drive delegation.
  • V3 (Week Strip) — compelling for tailors juggling multi-week deadlines (bridal, agbada). Only works if Order.deadline usage hits >60% of orders. Check Firestore first.
  • V4 (Hero Next Action) — opinionated. Can feel patronising when the tailor knows better than the algorithm.
  • V5 (Photo-First Gallery) — the differentiator. Tailors think in fabric, not status strings. Requires >50% fabric-photo upload rate; worth a feature-flagged Sprint 3+ experiment.
  • V6 (Finance-hybrid) — the pragmatic upgrade to V1. Ambient "₦320k this week" hook at the bottom gives the owner-operator side of a tailor's brain something to glance at, without cluttering the operational top. Ship together with Reports.

Suggested Sprint plan: (1) keep V1 shipped for now. (2) Spec the Reports tab — refactor Settings-placeholder into a real Reports destination, settings go into a profile menu. (3) When Reports lands, flip dashboard to V6 in the same release. (4) Revisit V5 once you know the fabric-photo upload rate from production.

V1 · Operational Stack SHIPPED

"Tell me what needs my attention, in priority order. Nothing else."

Pros
  • Names and garments are visible on the first screen — one fewer tap to recognise what needs doing
  • Cards self-hide when empty — a calm day genuinely looks calm, no "0 overdue" noise
  • Cheapest to maintain: no aggregates, no photos, no week computation
Cons
  • Can feel list-heavy when 3 buckets each have 3 rows — lots of vertical scroll
  • No at-a-glance view of how next week shapes up
  • Ignores that tailors think visually (fabric, silhouette) not textually
Fits when

Solo tailor, text-literate, deadline-driven workflow. Today's StitchPad target user.

V2 · Metric Grid + Today's List ALTERNATIVE

"Give me the numbers first, then the details. Like a POS back-office."

Pros
  • Ambient awareness — counts are visible without reading any rows
  • Unified today-list replaces three separate cards, less scrolling overall
  • Scales well as orders grow (10 overdue still takes the same tile space)
Cons
  • Counts without names means "tap to reveal" — extra hop vs V1 for the common case
  • Feels enterprise/POS — less warm, less personal for a one-person tailor shop
  • Status dots on a mixed list are less scannable than colour-grouped cards
Fits when

Multi-staff shop where tile counts drive delegation ("3 overdue, Chidi handle those"). Or when order volume is high enough that V1's row-per-order gets unwieldy.

V3 · Week Strip Timeline ALTERNATIVE

"Show me how my week shapes up, then zoom into today."

Pros
  • Forward visibility — tailor sees the Friday rush before Friday arrives
  • Natural for multi-week deadlines (bridal gowns, agbada) common in Nigerian tailoring
  • Makes planning conversations with clients easier ("I'm fully booked Fri, can it be Thu?")
Cons
  • Requires most orders to have deadline set — useless on orders without deadlines
  • The strip is visually busy; first-time users need to learn what the bars mean
  • Ready-for-pickup isn't naturally day-anchored, so it sits awkwardly in the schedule
Fits when

Workload is plannable days in advance, not just reactive. Check Firestore: what % of orders have a deadline set? If >60%, this variant earns its place. If not, V1 is better.

V4 · Hero Next Action ALTERNATIVE

"Don't make me decide — tell me the one thing to do next."

Pros
  • Zero decision fatigue — the app picks your next move for you
  • Large target for the most urgent order (accessibility + speed)
  • Works well for focused, single-track craftwork
Cons
  • Opinionated — can feel patronising when the tailor knows better than the algorithm
  • If the "most urgent" order isn't actually actionable (fabric hasn't arrived), the hero is wasted space
  • Hides context — you can't see everything at once, which some owners prefer
Fits when

The user trusts the app's prioritisation, workload is sequential (one piece at a time), and the business has clear urgency rules. Less good for tailors who batch-cut or juggle.

V5 · Photo-First Gallery DIFFERENTIATOR

"Tailors think in fabric and silhouette, not status strings."

Pros
  • Matches how tailors actually think — they remember "the burgundy corset for Fola" not "order #4201"
  • Leverages fabric photos already being uploaded on OrderItem — display change, not data change
  • Instagram-like feel is aspirational for a fashion brand owner, not just utilitarian
Cons
  • Needs orders to have photos — plain gradient placeholder is noticeably weaker than a real shot
  • Deadline state is a corner chip, smaller than V1/V3 — worse for reading at a glance
  • Takes more vertical space per order (photo + meta) so fewer fit on screen
Fits when

Fabric photo upload rate is >50% in production AND the target user values visual differentiation (brand-focused tailors vs pure operators). Worth a feature-flagged experiment in Sprint 3+.

V6 · Finance-hybrid PRAGMATIC UPGRADE

"Operational first, with an ambient line for the owner-operator side of the brain."

Pros
  • Identical to V1 above the fold — no regression in what's already working
  • One glance at "₦320k this week · +18%" answers the "how's business?" question without a tap
  • Tiny sparkline hints at trend direction; tap takes you to the real Reports screen
  • Bottom nav upgraded: Settings → Reports (Settings moves to a profile menu)
Cons
  • Only earns its place if the Reports tap destination actually exists — ship both together
  • Revenue numbers at the bottom of the list are easily missed on a busy day (may be fine — that's the point of "ambient")
  • Requires new weekly aggregation in the ViewModel (deliver the Reports screen anyway and it's free)
Fits when

Reports screen is also being shipped. This variant is the natural dashboard partner for a proper Reports tab — they should land in the same release, not separately.

V7 · Metric Grid + Reports hook V2 + V6 COMBINED

"POS-style counts first, then the rows, with an ambient finance line to close."

Pros
  • One screen, four answers: "how many need me?" (tiles), "who?" (list), "how's business?" (week card), "what's the next action?" (FAB)
  • Tiles scale with volume — 10 overdue takes no more vertical space than 1
  • Uses every pixel of the landing productively; no big empty spaces on a quiet day
  • Same bottom-nav swap as V6: Settings → Reports, Settings into profile menu
Cons
  • Highest information density of any variant — can feel cluttered for a first-time user
  • Counts-without-names means an extra tap when there's only 1 overdue (V1 wins that case)
  • Week card at the bottom is the 4th distinct section — some users may never scroll to it
  • Like V6, only earns its place if Reports is also shipped
Fits when

You want an at-a-glance, numbers-first dashboard and you're comfortable with higher density. Also: multi-staff shops where tile counts drive delegation conversations. Ship alongside the Reports tab, same release as V6 would have been.

V8 · Best of Both V1 + V2 HYBRID

"Tiles for the scan, color-accented rows for the warmth, a revenue line for ambient awareness."

Pros
  • From V2: 2×2 tile grid gives the at-a-glance state of the shop without reading any rows
  • From V1: each row has a coloured left accent bar + status chip — red/orange/green makes the urgency viscerally obvious, without needing three separate cards
  • From V6: bottom revenue hook with sparkline gives ambient finance awareness for the owner-operator brain
  • Outstanding is a tile (up top, small) — not its own row — so the list stays tight
  • Works at every volume: 1 overdue looks clean; 10 overdue collapses into "+ see all" without cluttering
Cons
  • Most information of any variant — tiles + list + week hook. Needs to feel curated, not crowded
  • Tile count (1) + row count (1 of that colour) is mildly redundant when volume is low
  • Week card earns its place only when Reports is shipped and gated per your freemium plan
Fits when

This is the "don't make me choose" answer. Tiles scan, accented rows warm, revenue hook hints. Ship with Reports; gate the revenue hook visibility on premium if you want to reserve anything for the paid tier.

V9 · Ship Target V2 + ALL V1 ADVANTAGES

"V2's tile-grid scannability plus every warmth V1 brought, minus the revenue hook — that's the V8 upgrade when Reports lands."

V1 advantages carried in
  • Self-hiding tiles — a tile disappears when its count is 0 so a calm day doesn't show "0 Overdue / 0 Due / 0 Ready". Not pictured here because the sample data has all buckets populated, but the logic exists in state already.
  • Welcome hero for brand-new users (0 orders AND 0 customers) — full-screen onboarding instead of an empty grid, CTA straight to "Add first customer"
  • All-clear banner when orders exist but every bucket is empty — "✨ All clear for today" instead of silence
  • Coloured left accent bar + status chip on every row — red "4d late", orange "Today", green "Ready" — V1's visceral urgency without the heavy three-card chrome
  • Names and garments on the landing — already in V2, kept
  • Cheap to maintain — no new aggregates, no photos, no week computation. Same state as the shipped V1.
V2 advantages kept
  • 2×2 tile grid for at-a-glance counts — the "dashboard feel" testers preferred
  • Unified list (no triple-card scrolling)
  • Scales: 10 overdue takes the same tile space as 1
  • Outstanding is a tile (not its own row) so the list stays tight
Upgrade path

V9 ships now. When the Reports tab lands (separate Notion task), V9 promotes to V8 — same layout, adds the bottom "₦320k this week · View reports →" card and swaps Settings → Reports in the bottom nav. Both changes happen in the Reports release.

Companion: the Reports tab

This is where sales metrics actually live. Not the dashboard. A proper Reports destination — replaces the Settings placeholder in the bottom nav; Settings moves to a profile menu. Period selector at the top, hero revenue for the range, daily trend, breakdown by garment, busiest days.

Reports tab FOLLOW-UP

"How is the business? For the period I care about."

What it answers
  • Revenue for the period — the number the owner really cares about, with a delta vs previous period (motivational + informative)
  • Daily trend — is revenue climbing or flat this month? Spot the busy/slow stretches at a glance
  • Cash reality — deposits collected vs still owed, so the owner knows what's in the bank vs on paper
  • Volume + avg ticket — distinguishes "lots of cheap orders" from "few expensive pieces"; informs pricing
  • Garment mix — which categories actually pay the bills. Often surprises tailors who think bridal drives revenue when it's really daily Agbada
  • Busiest days — staffing / studio hours planning. Fri+Sat is the expected pattern for Nigerian tailors
Implementation notes

Period selector (Week / Month / Quarter / Year) + custom range via the calendar icon top-right. Period aggregation runs client-side over the existing observeOrders flow — no new backend. Export icon top-right lets the tailor share a summary (useful for tax/bookkeeping). Can be gated as a paid-tier feature for the freemium model.

Nav change required

Current bottom nav: Dashboard · Customers · Orders · Settings. New: Dashboard · Customers · Orders · Reports. Settings moves to a profile menu on the Dashboard top-right (avatar tap).

Mockups use the same design tokens as DesignTokens.kt and the same fonts as the app. Sample data matches today's Firestore for Dan Ayo (Fola Sunday corset overdue, Bimbo Dann dress today/ready, ₦480k outstanding). Reports figures (₦1.84m/month, +18% vs March, top garments) are illustrative — actual numbers would come from observeOrders aggregation.
