Why this direction

  • · One hero treatment across all 3 screens — photo background (auth_background.webp) with logo badge + STITCHPAD + TAILORED FOR YOU lockup. Consistency = trust on auth flows.
  • · PNG → WebP conversion — 2.3 MB → 90 KB at 1024w q82 (≈26× smaller). For shipping to the iOS/Android bundle, generate a 384/512/768 webp set following the existing stitchpad_assets_webp/ pattern.
  • · Glow-halo on the logo badge — recreates the lit-from-behind effect from mockups #11/#12 so the white circle pops against the warm cloth.
  • · Brand mark sits on the photo, not under it — STITCHPAD black + TAILORED FOR YOU dim with a soft white text-shadow for legibility on the brighter saffron-cloth zones.
  • · Dark form card in both light + dark mode — premium auth treatment. Login/signup forms are typically theme-agnostic in this category (Stripe, Linear, Notion auth flows).
  • · WhatsApp = Required — explicit chip + green WhatsApp icon. The mockup with an opt-in toggle (#7) contradicted the new product requirement that this is the canonical contact channel for support and customer follow-up.
  • · No "Step 1 of 2" indicator (single step) — fair pushback from you: the indicator was speculative, with no defined step 2. Removed for now. If you want a multi-step flow later, three credible candidates for step 2 are listed below.
  • · Validation = format + self-attestation (V1) — reuses existing PhoneNormaliser.normaliseNigerianPhone(). Backend WhatsApp Cloud API verification is a future ticket once you've onboarded paying tailors.
  • · Sign in with Apple ships with Google, not after — App Store Review Guideline 4.8 requires Apple Sign-In as a peer to any third-party SSO on iOS. Submissions without it get rejected. Apple Developer Program enrollment ($99/yr + DUNS verification) is a prerequisite, not a feature decision — if it's blocking, ship email/password only until the dev account is ready.

If we ever want a Step 2 — three real candidates

  • · Measurement schema picker — tailor selects which body fields they collect (chest, waist, hip, sleeve, inseam, agbada length, etc.). Pre-configures the new-customer form they'll fill 50× a week. Highest product value; biggest design pass.
  • · Notification preferences — WhatsApp reminder cadence (daily/weekly), email opt-in. Sets up the WhatsApp pipeline the workshop step just declared canonical. Smallest scope, weakest hook for first-run.
  • · Subscription tier choice — Free (5 customers/mo) vs Premium ₦500 / ₦1000. Forces the conversion conversation upfront. Riskiest — too pushy on first run; better as a soft nudge later.
